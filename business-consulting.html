Understanding EMDR Therapy

How this revolutionary therapy works and why it's so effective

The Science Behind EMDR

EMDR is based on the so-called "adaptive information processing model" (AIP model). This assumes that our brain is fundamentally capable of processing stressful experiences itself - but this natural process can be blocked in the event of extreme stress or trauma experiences.

This is precisely where EMDR comes in: It helps to release such blockages so that traumatic memories can be processed and emotionally relieved. Typically, the therapist guides the client through targeted eye movements or alternating stimuli, while certain stressful memories are recalled at the same time.

The EMDR Process

This process takes place in several structured phases, including anamnesis and therapy planning, stabilization and preparation, targeted focus on stressful memories, desensitization and processing, strengthening positive beliefs, body awareness & integration, and conclusion and aftercare.

Scientific Evidence & Recognition

EMDR is backed by extensive research and official recognition worldwide

1
Meta-analysis by Rasines-Laudes & Serrano-Pintado (2023)

This systematic review analyzed 18 randomized controlled trials with a total of 1,213 participants. The results show that EMDR causes significant improvements in PTSD symptoms, anxiety and depression.

2
Expert opinion of the Scientific Advisory Board for Psychotherapy (WBP, 2014)

The WBP recognized EMDR as a scientifically sound psychotherapy method for adults with PTSD.

3
EMDR Institute - Overview of randomized studies

More than 20 randomized studies prove the effectiveness of EMDR in the treatment of trauma.

4
Cochrane review by Bisson et al. (2007)

This systematic review compared different psychological treatments for chronic PTSD and found EMDR to be an effective treatment option.

Official Recommendations

EMDR is recognized and recommended by leading health organizations worldwide

World Health Organization (WHO, 2013)

The WHO recommends EMDR as an evidence-based treatment for PTSD in adults.

American Psychiatric Association (APA, 2004)

The APA recognizes EMDR as an effective treatment for acute stress disorders and PTSD.

Federal Joint Committee (G-BA, 2014)

In Germany, EMDR was recognized in 2014 as a psychotherapy method for people with PTSD who have statutory health insurance.
